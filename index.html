About

I am an experimental physicist at NISER Bhubaneswar (Dept. of Atomic Energy), working at the intersection of nanofabrication, materials science, and advanced metrology.

My research spans thin-film growth, ion-beam processing, and SEM- and spectroscopy-based characterisation — with a focus on process diagnostics and defect analysis at the nanoscale.

I am completing my Integrated M.Sc. thesis on Au–Ge quantum dot nanostructures, investigating their formation dynamics and characterisation for optoelectronics and biomedical applications.

Outside the lab, I write about physics, science policy, and ideas at my blog Matter That Thinks.
