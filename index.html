About

I am a final-year Integrated M.Sc. Physics student at NISER Bhubaneswar, an autonomous institute under India's Department of Atomic Energy, graduating in July 2026.

Five years of research training have given me something most finance candidates lack: genuine quantitative rigour. I model complex systems, interpret noisy data, optimise under hard constraints, and reason about uncertainty — not as abstract skills but as daily practice.

I discovered finance during my undergraduate years through Zerodha Varsity and have since completed 8 Zerodha certifications, the SEBI-NISM Investor Certification, and a Foundation in Entrepreneurship (HBNI–IIMA Ventures). I track markets, read filings, and write about economics and finance at my blog.

I am preparing for CAT 2026 — not as a backup, but as a deliberate next step toward a career in finance, strategy, or consulting.

I am a final-year Integrated M.Sc. Physics student at NISER Bhubaneswar (Dept. of Atomic Energy), completing my thesis on Au–Ge nanoparticles synthesised via solid-state dewetting of bilayer thin films.

My research spans thin-film growth, ion-beam processing, and optical/structural characterisation — SEM, Raman, UV–Vis–NIR, PL, EDX, and cathodoluminescence. I care about process-property correlations and building a rigorous understanding of nanoscale behaviour.

I have interned at IIT Kanpur (plasma diagnostics) and across multiple labs at NISER, and I write about physics, science policy, and ideas at Matter That Thinks.
