Experience

M.Sc. Dissertation · 2024–2026 · Dr. Pratap Kumar Sahoo, NISER

Engineering Au–Ge Nanoparticles for Plasmonic Enhancement & Optoelectronic Applications

School of Physical Sciences · NISER Bhubaneswar

Investigated the formation and optoelectronic properties of Au–Ge nanoparticles synthesised via solid-state dewetting of Ge/Au bilayer thin films. Bilayers of 50 nm Ge with 10 nm and 20 nm Au were deposited on Si/SiO₂ and glass substrates and annealed between 400°C and 800°C. Observed a temperature-driven transition from continuous films to discrete near-spherical nanoparticles governed by surface diffusion and capillarity. EDX analysis revealed size-dependent phase behaviour — smaller particles forming alloys, larger particles forming phase-separated Janus-type structures. Optical measurements identified UV absorption from Ge interband transitions (E₂ critical point ~320 nm), visible LSPR of Au nanoparticles (~498–520 nm), and broad NIR absorption from free-carrier and hybrid alloy states. Raman confirmed crystalline Ge; PL was suppressed due to indirect bandgap and metallic quenching.

Research & Lab Work

Dec 2025 – Jan 2026

Ion Beam & Nanomaterials Lab · NISER

Winter Project — Raman & UV–Vis Characterisation

  • Performed Raman spectroscopy (532 nm, 633 nm) and UV–Vis–NIR spectroscopy on Au–Ge nanoparticles across annealing temperatures (400–800°C).
  • Identified sharp Ge–Ge optical phonon mode at ~300 cm⁻¹ confirming crystalline Ge; absence of amorphous Ge or GeOx signatures.
  • Attributed UV absorption at ~320 nm to E₂ critical-point interband transitions of Ge, with contributions from plasmon–semiconductor coupling.
  • Correlated spectral evolution with morphological changes observed in FESEM; documented structure–property relationships.
